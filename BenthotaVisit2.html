Benthota Hatchery

In Sri Lanka, there is a well-known turtle conservation initiative called the Bentota Turtle Hatchery. It is located in the well-known tourist attraction of Bentota, which is a town on Sri Lanka's southwest coast. As sea turtles face numerous risks in the wild, such as predation, pollution, habitat degradation, and illegal poaching, the main objective of the hatchery is to raise the survival rate of sea turtles. The effort is crucial in educating the public about the need to protect these threatened species.

Learn about the several sea turtle species that may be found in Sri Lanka at the Bentota Turtle Hatchery, including the Green Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. In order to safeguard sea turtles from both natural and human dangers, the hatchery harvests eggs from shoreline nesting areas that are particularly susceptible. The young turtles are nurtured once they hatch until they are a specific size and strength before being released into the ocean. Visitors to the hatchery can also take part in the release of baby turtles into the ocean. People can participate in conservation efforts and see the turtles return to their natural environment through this practical experience.
In addition to its conservation efforts, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery frequently works with nearby towns, schools, and organisations to spread the word about the value of preserving the maritime environment and sea turtle protection. Although the Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a well-liked tourist destination, there have been some questions raised about the procedures and working conditions at several turtle hatcheries in Sri Lanka. It's always a good idea to do some research and pick trustworthy businesses that put the welfare and conservation of sea turtles first.





A Look into the Bentota Turtle Hatchery

Everyone who comes to Bentota enjoys visiting the Bentota Turtle Hatchery Project. The picturesque golden sands that entice you to our island also offer numerous endangered turtle species, such as the Olive Ridley and Leatherback turtles, an alluring nesting site. The project's objective is to preserve and safeguard the eggs against predators and other threats. To find out more about these beautiful and endangered sea creatures, go to the turtle hatchery in Bentota. Until the eggs hatch and are prepared to swim back home, the turtle hatchery guards the eggs. The group also saves sea turtles who sustain injuries from fishing nets, motors, and other sources while at sea. Before being released back, they are frequently rehabbed.






The way to Benthota Turtle Hatchery

To reach Bentota Turtle Hatchery, you can follow these general directions and also It's always a good idea to have the exact address and contact information of the Bentota Turtle Hatchery,

  • International Travel: If you are traveling from outside Sri Lanka, you can book a flight to Bandaranaike International Airport (also known as Colombo Airport) in Colombo, Sri Lanka. Then you have a few options to reach Bentota Turtle Hatchery:

  • Private Taxi: The most convenient option is to hire a private taxi or cab from the airport. There are several taxi services available at the airport, and you can easily find one at the designated taxi counters. Inform the driver that you want to go to Bentota Turtle Hatchery, and they will take you directly there.

  • Public Transportation: Another option is to take a train or bus from Colombo to Bentota. From the airport, you can take a taxi or a tuk-tuk to the nearest train or bus station.

  • Train: Take a taxi or tuk-tuk to Colombo Fort Railway Station, which is the main railway station in Colombo. From there, you can catch a train to Bentota Railway Station. Once you reach Bentota, you can hire a taxi or tuk-tuk to reach the Turtle Hatchery.

  • Bus: Take a taxi or tuk-tuk to the Colombo Bastian Mawatha Bus Terminal. From there, you can find buses heading towards Bentota. Look for buses with "Bentota" or "Aluthgama" as their destination. Ask the conductor or locals to guide you to the correct bus. Once you reach Bentota, you can hire a taxi or tuk-tuk to reach the Turtle Hatchery.

  • Specific Location: The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is located in Induruwa, which is a village close to Bentota. You can ask the driver to take you to the Bentota Turtle Hatchery in Induruwa. It's a popular tourist destination, so most drivers should be familiar with the place.




Important details concerning the Bentota Turtle Hatchery


  • Conservation Efforts:The Olive Ridley, Green, Loggerhead, Hawksbill, and Leatherback turtles, among other species of sea turtles, are among those whose conservation is greatly aided by the hatchery. Its main objectives are the gathering and safeguarding of sea turtle eggs, their controlled hatching, and the safe release of the hatchlings into the ocean.

  • Turtle Rehabilitation: The hatchery offers a sanctuary for sea turtles that have been hurt or are otherwise unable to survive. Until they are ready to be released back into the wild, these turtles get care and rehabilitation at the facility.

  • Visitor Experience: Visitors who are interested in learning more about sea turtles and conservation are welcome at the Bentota Turtle Hatchery. Visitors can witness the eggs in the hatchery, watch the newborn turtles being released into the sea, and interact with the resident turtles at the centre to observe the various stages of the turtle breeding process.

  • Educational Programs:To raise awareness of turtle protection among residents and visitors, the hatchery runs educational programmes and initiatives. They stress how crucial it is to safeguard sea turtles and their habitats, lessen pollution, and implement sustainable practises.

  • Donations and Volunteering: To sustain its conservation activities, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery is dependent on donations and the help of volunteers. Visitors can help by giving money or giving up their time to help with various tasks at the hatchery.

    • While the hatchery is a well-liked tourist destination, there has been some discussion and criticism over particular elements of sea turtle conservation facilities in Sri Lanka. Some contend that some turtle hatcheries put tourism before conservation and might even use methods that aren't fully good for the turtles. If you're interested in aiding Sri Lanka's attempts to conserve sea turtles, it's a good idea to do some research and pick trustworthy conservation organisations.
