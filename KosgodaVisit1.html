Kosgoda Hatchery

It is located near the coastal community of Kosgoda, which is about 70 kilometres south of Colombo, Sri Lanka's capital. In order to combat the fall in sea turtle populations brought on by many threats, such as habitat destruction, pollution, and poaching, the hatchery was constructed. It actively contributes to turtle conservation efforts and plays a critical role in bringing attention to how important sea turtle conservation is.
The Green Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle are just a few of the sea turtle species that may be found in Sri Lanka. The hatchery's staff runs educational activities and disseminates knowledge about the life cycle and behaviour of sea turtles.The gathering and incubation of sea turtle eggs is one of the key tasks of the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery. Turtle eggs are susceptible to being damaged or harvested and are frequently brought to the hatchery by locals and fishermen.
The hatchery offers a more secure setting for the eggs to develop since it shields them from outside predators and human interference. Visitors can see the newborn turtles being released into the ocean once the eggs hatch. This is a chance to support the conservation of these threatened species while also having a wonderful experience. Injured or ill turtles are also treated at the hatchery before being released back into their natural habitat after they have healed.










The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery – Sri Lanka’s Part in Turtle Conservation

One of 18 turtle hatcheries along Sri Lanka's southern coast is the Turtle Care Centre at Kosgoda Beach. The institution was founded in 1981 and is situated in the coastal community of Kosgoda in South Western coastal village. Additionally, Kosgoda serves as the nesting ground for all five species of turtles that come to Sri Lanka. This is not the case with many of the other island-area hatcheries, where just some of the species are present on the beaches. The facility primarily serves as a hatchery and makes great efforts to boost the rate of hatching and survival of young turtles. However, the Care Centre also treats and releases sick or injured adults back into the ocean after treating them.
They also organise awareness campaigns for both domestic and foreign tourists and volunteer programmes.The Kosgoda Turtle Care Centre is a wonderful place to enjoy yourself while learning about and appreciating nature. It is interactive and family-friendly, so you should absolutely go when you're on vacation in Sri Lanka.Take advantage of the Kosgoda Turtle Care Centre visit as part of your South Seas tour with Lakpura. a chance to observe sea turtles of different species during various phases of development. Volunteer or assist with turtle feeding to get up close and personal with these fascinating animals. By making a donation to support the turtles' needs, you may contribute to conservation.





The steps taken by the hatchery are as follows:

The staff searches the beach at night for female sea turtles that emerge from the water to lay eggs. Until the mother has finished laying the eggs and has returned to the ocean, they guard the mother from a distance while she is in the nesting stage. To keep the eggs safe from predators and careless human behaviour, the eggs are moved from the beach to the hatchery.
Fishermen are additionally compensated for bringing any turtle eggs they locate to the hatchery.In a safe environment, the eggs grow and hatch. It can take 45 to 60 days to complete. If there are no issues, the newly hatched turtles are released into the water that same day. This is done in an effort to limit human influence on their way of life as much as possible.




Where Located & How to Reach Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

On Sri Lanka's southwest coast, near the village of Kosgoda, is where you may find the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery. It is located roughly 76 kilometres (47 miles) south of Colombo, Sri Lanka's capital.There are several ways to go to the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery,


  • By Air: If you're travelling from another nation or city, you can fly into Sri Lanka's Bandaranaike International Airport (also known as Colombo Airport). From there, you can take a planned transfer to Kosgoda or hire a cab. Depending on how congested the roads are, the drive takes 2 to 3 hours.

  • By Train: Taking the train from Colombo to Kosgoda is an additional alternative. From Colombo Fort Railway Station to Kosgoda Station, trains run frequently. The trip takes about two to three hours, and once you get to Kosgoda, you have the option of taking a tuk-tuk or walking to the turtle hatchery.

  • By Road: Travelling here by road is a breeze. If you're already in Sri Lanka, you can travel from Colombo to Kosgoda by bus or by cab. You have two choices for getting to the turtle hatchery: the Colombo-Galle A2 highway or the E01 Southern Express motorway. It takes 2 to 3 hours to travel by vehicle.Between Colombo and Galle, buses run frequently, and Kosgoda is situated along this route.
